# Phase 0: Design Challenge

> Goal: before any research or ideation, scope the challenge, assemble the right people, and prepare. Phase 0 is the setup that determines whether everything downstream is aimed at the right target.

Skipping Phase 0 is the most common reason design thinking efforts drift. A well-framed challenge and a prepared team save weeks of wandering later.

---

## Tools & methods in this phase

- **Scoping** — define the boundaries of the challenge: what's in, what's out.
- **Preparation** — logistics, materials, timeline, and workshop planning.
- **Multidisciplinary team** — assemble diverse perspectives; homogeneous teams produce narrow solutions.
- **Concept map** — visually map what you already know about the space and how concepts relate.
- **Stakeholder map** — identify everyone with a stake in the outcome and their influence/interest.
- **Charetting** — an intensive collaborative kickoff session to align the team early.

---

## Where AI genuinely helps

**1. First-draft scoping**
Feed AI the challenge and get a proposed scope with in/out boundaries to react to and refine.

**2. Concept mapping**
AI can rapidly generate a first-pass concept map of a domain you're entering, which you then correct with real knowledge.

**3. Stakeholder identification**
Ask AI to brainstorm stakeholder categories you might have overlooked (regulators, adjacent teams, indirect users).

---

## Where AI will mislead you

- AI can't assemble your team or read your org's politics. Stakeholder *influence* is contextual and human.
- A confident-looking scope is not a validated one. Phase 0 outputs are starting hypotheses, not decisions.

---

## Ready-to-use prompts

**Prompt: Draft a challenge scope**
```
Our design challenge is: [describe]. 
Propose a scope: what's explicitly IN scope, what's OUT, and what's 
ambiguous and needs a decision. Flag where the challenge might be 
framed too broadly or too narrowly.
```

**Prompt: Stakeholder map starter**
```
For this challenge: [describe], list likely stakeholders grouped by 
influence (high/low) and interest (high/low). Include non-obvious ones 
I might overlook. Mark which I should engage early vs. keep informed.
```

**Prompt: Concept map starter**
```
Give me a first-pass concept map for the domain of [topic]: the key 
concepts, sub-concepts, and how they relate. I'll correct it with 
real domain knowledge, so flag anything you're unsure about.
```

---

## Before entering the Problem Space, check:

- [ ] Is the challenge scoped, do I know what's in and out?
- [ ] Do I have a multidisciplinary team, or am I about to design in an echo chamber?
- [ ] Have I mapped stakeholders and their influence?
- [ ] Is the team aligned on the challenge, not just individually briefed?
