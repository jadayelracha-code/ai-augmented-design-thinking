# Pitch: Stakeholder Presentation

> Goal: turn everything you learned and built into a compelling, honest case that moves stakeholders to act. The best solution dies in a drawer if you can't get others to believe in it.

Pitch is where design thinking meets persuasion. After all the rigor, empathy, and testing, you now need to make people *care*. The trap is dishonesty-by-polish: dressing up a half-validated idea as a sure thing. A great pitch is persuasive AND truthful about what's proven versus assumed.

> 🎤 Persuade with evidence, not just enthusiasm. Your credibility is the asset.

---

## Tools & methods in this phase

- **Unique Value Proposition (UVP)** — the single, clear reason this solution matters to its users, and why it's different.
- **Storytelling** — frame the journey (user's problem → insight → solution → evidence) as a narrative people remember.
- **Elevator pitch** — the whole case, compressed into 30-60 seconds.

---

## Where AI genuinely helps

**1. Crafting and sharpening the UVP**
AI is strong at compressing a fuzzy value proposition into crisp candidate one-liners to choose between.

**2. Structuring the narrative**
Feed it your journey and evidence; get a story arc (problem, stakes, insight, solution, proof) to refine.

**3. Drafting the elevator pitch**
AI produces multiple tight variations at different lengths and tones fast.

**4. Anticipating tough questions**
Ask AI to role-play a skeptical stakeholder and pressure-test your pitch before the real room does.

---

## Where AI will mislead you

- **AI writes persuasive copy that can outrun your evidence.** It'll happily claim "users love it" when you tested with five people. Keep claims tied to what you actually proved.
- Generic pitch language ("revolutionary," "seamless," "game-changing") is AI's default and stakeholders' cue to stop believing you. Cut it.
- It can craft a story so clean it hides the real uncertainty, which destroys credibility the moment someone probes. Build in honest acknowledgment of what's still assumption.

## Ready-to-use prompts

**Prompt: Sharpen the UVP**
```
Our solution: [describe]. Users' core job/pain/gain: [paste]. What we proved in
testing: [paste evidence]. Draft 5 candidate Unique Value Propositions — one
crisp sentence each — that are specific and honest (no hype words like
"revolutionary" or "seamless"). Note which are best supported by our evidence.
```

**Prompt: Structure the story**
```
Help me structure a pitch narrative from this journey: [problem, insight,
solution, test evidence]. Arc: the user's problem and stakes, the insight we
found, our solution, and the PROOF. Keep claims tied to actual evidence — flag
anywhere I'd be overclaiming.
```

**Prompt: Draft the elevator pitch**
```
Compress this into a 30-second elevator pitch, then a 60-second version:
[paste UVP + key evidence]. Specific and credible, not hype. Lead with the user
problem, not the solution.
```

**Prompt: Pressure-test with a skeptic**
```
Role-play a skeptical stakeholder (limited budget, seen many pitches fail).
Here's my pitch: [paste]. Ask me the 5 hardest questions — about evidence,
feasibility, and viability — that you'd ask before committing resources.
```

---

## Before you present, check:

- [ ] Is my UVP a single, specific, honest sentence, no hype words?
- [ ] Does my story lead with the USER'S problem, not our clever solution?
- [ ] Is every claim tied to evidence I can actually back up?
- [ ] Have I been honest about what's proven vs. still assumption?
- [ ] Can I survive the hardest skeptical questions?
- [ ] Would the users I studied recognize themselves in this pitch?
